// Need-to-know rules for Hyderabad roads
🏍️ TWO WHEELERS
Helmet mandatory for rider & pillion. Fine: ₹1000 (1st offence). Riding without licence: ₹5000.
🚗 SPEED LIMITS
City roads: 40–50 km/h. Highways: 80 km/h for cars. ORR: 100 km/h max. School zones: 20 km/h.
🍺 DRUNK DRIVING
BAC limit: 30 mg/100ml. Fine: ₹10,000 + up to 6 months imprisonment. Zero tolerance enforced.
📱 MOBILE USAGE
Using phone while driving: ₹5000. Repeat offence: ₹10,000. Even hands-free is restricted while moving.
🚦 SIGNAL JUMPING
Jumping red light: ₹5000 + possible imprisonment. CCTV enforcement active across Hyderabad.
🅿️ NO PARKING
Parking on yellow lines or bus stops: ₹500 + towing charges up to ₹2000 in GHMC zones.
🪑 SEATBELT
Mandatory for all passengers. Fine: ₹1000 per person. Children under 12 must sit in the back seat.
🚸 SCHOOL ZONES
Speed limit 20 km/h near schools from 7–9 AM and 2–4 PM. Traffic wardens deployed.
📝 DOCUMENTS
Always carry: DL, RC Book, Insurance, PUC Certificate. DigiLocker is accepted as valid proof.

ABOUT
// Why Traffic Rules Hyd exists

Traffic Rules Hyd is a pixelated, interactive guide to road rules and safety in Hyderabad, Telangana.

Hyderabad's roads handle over 70 lakh vehicles daily. Knowing the rules isn't just legal — it's survival.

From TSRTC routes to ORR flyovers, from Secunderabad junctions to Gachibowli — this guide covers it all.

Built for students, new drivers, and everyday commuters of the City of Pearls. 🏗️
